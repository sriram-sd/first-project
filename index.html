Our Services

Rose

Description: Roses come in a variety of colors, each symbolizing different emotions. Red roses are typically associated with love, while white roses symbolize purity. Cultivation: They thrive in well-drained soil and require plenty of sunlight. Pruning is essential for healthy growth. Uses: Commonly used in bouquets, gardens, and as ornamental plants. They also have significance in literature and art.

Lily

Description: Lilies are known for their large, fragrant blooms and come in many varieties, including Asiatic, Oriental, and Trumpet lilies. They often symbolize purity and refined beauty. Cultivation: They prefer well-drained soil and can grow in full sun or partial shade. Proper spacing is crucial for good airflow. Uses: Often used in floral arrangements, they are also popular in gardens and can carry significant cultural and symbolic meanings.

Sunflower

Description: Sunflowers are tall plants with large, round yellow petals and a brown center that houses seeds. They are known for their ability to turn toward the sun (heliotropism). Uses: Besides being ornamental, sunflower seeds are edible and used in snacks and cooking, while sunflower oil is a common cooking oil.

Tulip

Description: Tulips are bulbous perennials with smooth, cup-shaped flowers. They come in nearly every color and are often seen in spring gardens. Cultivation: They prefer well-drained soil and a sunny location. Tulip bulbs need a cold period to bloom successfully. Uses: Popular in floral arrangements and festivals, especially in places like the Netherlands, where tulip fields attract tourists.
